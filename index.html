Michelangelo Falco

Linguist · PhD Scuola Normale Superiore, Pisa

I am a linguist working on the syntax-semantics interface in Romance and Germanic languages. I was a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Fellow (Career Restart) at the Leibniz-Centre General Linguistics (ZAS), Berlin (2019–2023), and a postdoctoral fellow at the Center for Mind/Brain Sciences (CIMeC), University of Trento (2012–2014). My doctoral studies were at Scuola Normale Superiore, Pisa, with research stays at UCLA and the University of Geneva.

My work established that discourse-linked nounless DPs involve covert partitive structures whose availability depends on common ground, and produced the first typology of partitive constructions with null operators. I guest-edited the Glossa special collection Partitives and Partitivity (2019), coordinating contributions across six languages. My study of Italian proportional determiners (fractions, percentages) identified five morpho-syntactic factors governing their interpretation.

My current research focuses on the lexical semantics of half-words (metà, mezzo, half): how grammar gates which partition readings are accessible and how common ground selects among them. This work, developed with Roberto Zamparelli, proposes an A–G partition framework that maps syntactic frames to reading families across Italian and English.
