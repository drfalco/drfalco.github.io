Michelangelo Falco

Linguist · PhD Scuola Normale Superiore, Pisa

I am an independent researcher in linguistics. Previously, I was a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Fellow at the Leibniz-Centre General Linguistics (ZAS), Berlin (2019–2023), and a postdoctoral fellow at the Center for Mind/Brain Sciences (CIMeC), University of Trento (2012–2014).

My research concerns the syntax-semantics interface, with a focus on how common ground and grammar jointly determine the interpretation of partition expressions. I study the lexical semantics of half-words (metà, mezzo, half, Hälfte) across Romance, English, and German, combining formal semantics, corpus methods, and behavioral experiments.

Earlier work addressed partitive constructions, proportional determiners, specificity, and weak crossover.
